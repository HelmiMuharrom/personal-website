It's Funny How Things Change as We Grow Up

When we were children, we did things simply because we enjoyed them. We didn't overthink about our passion, identity, or the long-term implications. We did things for the pure joy of it, without any self-imposed expectations.

But as we become adults, we start to question ourselves more. We think about passion, identity, and whether our actions align with our desired future. We become concerned about societal norms and expectations. Passion becomes a focal point of our decisions, as we constantly wonder if what we're doing is what we truly want or if it will lead us to a successful life.

I'm not blaming maturity or the natural development of our minds. It's just how our minds work. But it's essential to remember that we were more free when we didn't constantly question who we really are.

Sometimes We Need to Learn Something New

Let's imagine you're an adult who has been working at the same place for years. You've become accustomed to the routine and know your way around. Then, one day, something happens: Your boss asks for changes in how things are done.

At first, you may not like this change because it means learning something newâ€”and who wants more work? But what if I told you that learning something new can actually be fun? And even more importantly, what if I told you that failing at learning new things is good for your brain?

Sometimes We Need to Be Simple

Sometimes, we need to simplify.

Sometimes, we need to do something easy.

Sometimes, we need to do something that is not complicated and requires no effort from our brains at all.

And sometimes, this can be as simple as learning something new.

Conclusion:

There is nothing wrong with your adult life.
